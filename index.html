Studio Ghibli Films

Japan's version of magical Disney films. Each film is distinct from each other with its own theme and personality while still feeling "familiar".

Spirited Away

By Hayao Miyazaki

Spirited Away was awarded the best animated feature in 2003 by the Oscars. During her family's move to the suburbs, a sullen 10-year-old girl wanders into a world ruled by gods, witches and spirits, a world where humans are changed into beasts. This film is magical and might make no sense but that is the essence of most Ghibli films. While there are common themes and philosophies in play, the visuals are breathtaking and characters are fleshed out.

Princess Mononoke

By Hayao Miyazaki

Princess Mononoke is a brilliantly layered look at a complicated conflict. There are many themes in play like war, the importance of our environment, and the relationship between human and nature. It is gory and one of the most mature films in the Ghibli collection. Like the last movie, it is also magical and thrive off a fantasy world with sprits.

Castle in the Sky

By Hayao Miyazaki

Castle in the Sky is a film about how it would be if a sky island existed and it was revealed to you that it was a world ending weapon. The themes in this movie is exploration, discovery, human greed and morality. The atmsophere and the world building of this film is off the charts while we learn about different faction's purpose for seeking the sky island.
